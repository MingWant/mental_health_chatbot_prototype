RAG Sample Document Templates (English)

This file provides simple templates to test each chunking strategy.

1) Fixed-length chunking (generic text)

Title: Tips for Better Sleep

Content:
- Sleep affects mood, focus, and learning.
- Keep a consistent sleep schedule.
- Limit caffeine after noon.
- Create a dark, cool, and quiet bedroom.
- Turn off screens 60 minutes before bed.
- If you cannot sleep in 20 minutes, get up and do a calming activity.

Paste the bullet list as a single paragraph to see fixed-size chunks.

2) Semantic chunking (sentences/paragraphs)

Title: Managing Exam Anxiety

Paragraph 1:
Exam anxiety is common among students. It can show up as worry, restlessness, racing thoughts, or trouble sleeping.

Paragraph 2:
Useful techniques include slow breathing, grounding, and scheduling short, focused study blocks.

Paragraph 3:
When anxiety interferes with daily life for more than two weeks, consider talking to a counselor.

3) Session chunking (dialogue/transcript)

Meeting Transcript: Stress Management Workshop

Speaker 1: Welcome everyone. Today we will share quick stress relief techniques.
Speaker 2: When I feel stressed, I start with four-count breathing.
Speaker 1: Great. Any other quick tips?
Speaker 3: A short walk helps me reset after long study sessions.

4) Hierarchical chunking (headings/sections)

# Student Mental Health Guide

## 1. Understanding Emotions
Text about recognizing feelings and early signs of stress.

### 1.1 Anxiety
Symptoms, short-term coping skills, and when to seek help.

### 1.2 Low Mood
Daily routine, social connection, and professional support.

## 2. Stress Management
Practical steps for time management and breaks.

### 2.1 Time Management
Prioritize tasks, use a timer, and schedule rest.

5) Adaptive chunking (mixed structure)

Intro paragraph about wellness.

Speaker 1: I sometimes overthink before exams.

## 1. Grounding Techniques
Short list of techniques.

A concluding paragraph about self-compassion.

Tips:
- For hierarchical chunking on English docs, include Markdown headings like #, ##, or numbered headings like 1., 1.1.
- For session chunking, include clear speaker tags ("Speaker 1:") or timestamps.
